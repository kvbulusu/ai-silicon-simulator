📘 Chapter 8: Power & Thermal Management

Exercise 8.1: DVFS Trade-off Analysis

Scenario: Your datacenter has limited cooling capacity (600W per GPU). The H100 default TDP is 700W.

Task:

  1. Load the H100 preset
  2. Reduce frequency from 1980 MHz to 1400 MHz
  3. Observe the TDP change
  4. Calculate the performance loss vs power savings

Question: What is the performance-per-watt improvement?

Solution:

Expected Results:

  • TDP: 700W → ~520W (26% reduction)
  • Performance: 1979 TFLOPS → ~1400 TFLOPS (29% reduction)
  • Perf/Watt: 2.83 → 2.69 TFLOPS/W (5% worse)

Key Insight: DVFS follows cubic power law (P ∝ V²f). Frequency reduction saves power but hurts efficiency. This is the "DVFS trap" discussed in Chapter 8.

Better Approach: Keep frequency high, use power gating or reduce active cores instead.

Exercise 8.2: Power Iceberg Calculation

Scenario: Your datacenter has PUE of 1.6 (typical for older facilities).

Task:

  1. Calculate chip-to-grid power multiplier
  2. For 8× H100 cluster, what's the grid power draw?
  3. What's the annual electricity cost at $0.12/kWh?
Solution:

Calculation:

  • Chip: 700W (H100 TDP)
  • × 1.08 (VRM losses) = 756W at server input
  • × 1.10 (PSU losses) = 832W at rack PDU
  • × 1.60 (PUE) = 1,331W at grid

8× GPU Cluster: 1,331W × 8 = 10.6 kW grid power

Annual Cost: 10.6 kW × 8,760 hrs × $0.12/kWh = $11,155/year

Key Insight: Your 700W chip actually costs you 1.9× at the grid (1,331W). Improving PUE from 1.6 → 1.3 saves $2,600/year per 8-GPU cluster.

📘 Chapter 10: Memory Subsystems

Exercise 10.1: Memory Wall Diagnosis

Scenario: Your LLaMA 3 70B inference is running slower than expected on H100.

Task:

  1. Load H100 + LLaMA 3 70B preset
  2. Check arithmetic intensity (should be ~0.12 for decode)
  3. Identify the bottleneck
  4. Calculate theoretical speedup from HBM3 → HBM3e
Solution:

Analysis:

  • Arithmetic Intensity: 0.12 (memory-bound decode phase)
  • Roofline: Well below compute ceiling, on memory BW line
  • Bottleneck: HBM bandwidth (3.35 TB/s on H100)

Upgrade to H200 (HBM3e @ 4.8 TB/s):

  • Speedup: 4.8 / 3.35 = 1.43× (43% faster)
  • Cost increase: ~18%
  • ROI: Excellent for memory-bound workloads

Key Insight: For inference decode (autoregressive), you're bottlenecked by memory bandwidth, not compute. This is the "Memory Wall" — upgrading compute (more cores) won't help, but faster memory will.

📘 Chapter 14: Agentic AI

Exercise 14.1: Agentic vs Traditional Economics

Scenario: You're sizing a datacenter for 1M queries/day.

Task:

  1. Calculate GPUs needed for Traditional Inference (1.2s/query)
  2. Calculate GPUs needed for Agentic AI (7.2s/query)
  3. What's the infrastructure cost difference?
Solution:

Traditional Inference:

  • 1M queries/day = 11.6 queries/sec
  • At 1.2s/query: Need 11.6 × 1.2 = 13.9 ≈ 14 GPUs
  • Cost: 14 × $4,200 = $58,800

Agentic AI:

  • At 7.2s/query: Need 11.6 × 7.2 = 83.5 ≈ 84 GPUs
  • But GPU utilization is only 21%, so effective: 84 / 0.21 = 400 GPUs worth of infrastructure
  • Cost: 84 × $4,200 = $352,800

Cost Difference: $352,800 vs $58,800 = 6× more expensive

Key Insight: Agentic AI inverts datacenter economics. You need 4× more CPUs and your GPUs idle 79% of the time during orchestration/tool-calls. But you gain multi-step reasoning, planning, and tool use capabilities.

Tradeoff: 6× higher cost, 10× more capable. ROI depends on task value.

📘 Chapter 16: Custom Silicon

Exercise 16.1: Build vs Buy Decision

Scenario: You're a startup considering custom silicon for your AI accelerator product.

Given:

  • NRE (Silicon + Software): $80M
  • Unit cost: $600 (custom) vs $4,200 (H100)
  • Performance: 1.3× better than H100

Task: Calculate break-even volume

Solution:

Calculation:

  • Savings per unit: $4,200 - $600 = $3,600
  • Break-even volume: $80M / $3,600 = 22,222 units
  • At 10K units/year → 2.2 years to break even

But wait — Software Risk:

  • NVIDIA has 15 years of CUDA ecosystem ($2B+ investment)
  • Your $80M includes only basic software stack
  • Customer adoption with immature software: ~25%
  • Effective break-even: 22,222 / 0.25 = 88,888 units
  • At 10K units/year → 8.9 years (most startups fail before this)

Decision:

  • ✅ BUILD if: Volume > 100K/year, or willing to invest $200M+ in software
  • ❌ BUY if: Volume < 50K/year, need fast time-to-market

Key Insight: Hardware cost is easy to calculate. Software ecosystem maturity is the hidden killer. This is why Groq, Graphcore, and others struggled despite superior silicon.
